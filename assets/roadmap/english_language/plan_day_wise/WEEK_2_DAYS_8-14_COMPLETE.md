## ğŸ—£ï¸ STEP 3: SHADOW SPEAKING - Professional Questions (15 minutes)

**Now it's YOUR turn to practice asking questions!**

### Part A: Learn 8 Essential Professional Questions (10 minutes)

**Practice each question 3 times with proper intonation:**

**1. "Could you please explain this to me?"**
- **When to use:** You don't understand something
- **Tone:** Polite, humble
- **Practice:** Say it 3 times, making your voice go UP at the end â†—ï¸
- **Example situation:** "Could you please explain this API documentation to me?"

**2. "I'm not sure I understand. Can you clarify?"**
- **When to use:** Something is confusing or unclear
- **Tone:** Honest, seeking help
- **Practice:** 3 times with rising tone
- **Example:** "I'm not sure I understand this requirement. Can you clarify?"

**3. "What do you mean by that?"**
- **When to use:** Someone said something and you need more explanation
- **Tone:** Curious, engaged
- **Practice:** 3 times, emphasize "mean"
- **Example:** "What do you mean by 'refactor the code'?"

**4. "Could you say that again, please?"**
- **When to use:** You didn't hear clearly or need to hear it again
- **Tone:** Polite, attentive
- **Practice:** 3 times, slowly and clearly
- **Example:** "Sorry, could you say that requirement again, please?"

**5. "Can you show me how to do this?"**
- **When to use:** You need a demonstration or practical example
- **Tone:** Eager to learn
- **Practice:** 3 times with enthusiasm
- **Example:** "Can you show me how to deploy this application?"

**6. "I need help with this task. Could you assist me?"**
- **When to use:** You're stuck and need someone's help
- **Tone:** Direct but polite
- **Practice:** 3 times confidently
- **Example:** "I need help debugging this error. Could you assist me?"

**7. "What's the best way to approach this?"**
- **When to use:** You want advice or guidance on how to do something
- **Tone:** Seeking wisdom, respectful
- **Practice:** 3 times thoughtfully
- **Example:** "What's the best way to approach this optimization problem?"

**8. "Could you give me some guidance on this?"**
- **When to use:** You need direction or suggestions
- **Tone:** Professional, open to learning
- **Practice:** 3 times with respect
- **Example:** "Could you give me some guidance on writing better unit tests?"

### Question 2: "How do you handle disagreements with team members?"

**This tests:** Teamwork, communication, conflict resolution, professionalism

**Practice Answer (customize for YOUR style):**

"I believe that disagreements in a team are natural and can actually be healthy if handled professionally. When I disagree with a team member, I follow a specific approach to resolve it constructively.

First, I listen carefully to understand their perspective completely. I don't interrupt or judge immediately. I ask clarifying questions to make sure I fully understand their reasoning. Often, what seems like a disagreement is actually a miscommunication or different understanding of the requirements.

Second, I express my opinion clearly but politely and respectfully. I use phrases like 'I understand your point, but have you considered...' or 'I see it differently because...' Instead of saying 'You're wrong,' I focus on the problem, not the person. I explain my reasoning with specific examples or data when possible.

Third, I try to find common ground. Usually, both sides want the same end goal - we just have different ideas about how to get there. I look for aspects we both agree on and build from there.

Fourth, I suggest we examine the options objectively. I might propose: 'Let's list the pros and cons of each approach' or 'Can we create a small proof of concept to test both ideas?' Data and evidence help move the discussion from opinions to facts.

Fifth, I'm open to compromise. Sometimes the best solution combines ideas from both perspectives. I'm willing to adjust my position if the other person makes good points that I hadn't considered.

Finally, if we still can't agree, I'm comfortable asking a senior developer, team lead, or manager for their opinion. There's no shame in seeking guidance from someone with more experience.

For example, recently my colleague Fahad and I disagreed about database choice for a new microservice. He wanted to use MongoDB because of flexibility, but I thought PostgreSQL was better for our use case because we needed strong ACID properties and complex queries. 

Instead of arguing, we sat down and listed our requirements clearly. We discussed the pros and cons of each database for those specific requirements. We also created a small prototype to test query performance with both databases. After testing and discussion, we realized that PostgreSQL was indeed better for our specific needs because we had many relational queries. Fahad agreed with the data, and we moved forward with PostgreSQL.

The project was successful, and Fahad and I maintained a good working relationship because we handled the disagreement professionally and focused on finding the best solution rather than 'winning' the argument.

The key lessons I've learned about handling disagreements are: respect different opinions, focus on problems not people, use data and evidence, be open to compromise, and maintain professional relationships even when you disagree."
